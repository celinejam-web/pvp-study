ACVIM Forum 2026 · Seattle · Poster Presentation

Effect of Trazodone on PR Interval in Healthy Dogs

Shin-Ling Jan  ·  Huai-Hsun Hsu  ·  Wen-Ying Chen

Department of Veterinary Medicine, College of Veterinary Medicine, National Chung Hsing University

Oral trazodone increased heart rate and shortened the PR interval in healthy conscious dogs; the decrease in PR interval was not fully explained by the increase in heart rate.

Background Methods Population Results Clinical Implication

01 · Background & Objectives

Why this study?

  • Oral trazodone is widely used as a pre-visit anxiolytic in dogs, including in cardiology patients where parenteral sedatives may confound cardiac examination
  • Human case reports document AV conduction block following trazodone overdose, drug interactions, or in patients with pre-existing cardiovascular disease
  • Prior canine studies confirm trazodone increases heart rate and reduces heart rate variability
  • Whether trazodone directly affects AV conduction in conscious dogs at clinical doses remained unknown

Objectives

  • Does oral trazodone (8 mg/kg) alter PR interval in conscious healthy dogs?
  • Is any PR change driven by heart rate, or does an HR-independent AV conduction effect exist?

02 · Methods

Study Design

Design
Prospective, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled crossover trial
Animals
18 healthy adult client-owned dogs; screened by physical examination, CBC, biochemistry, echocardiography, and ECG
Protocol
Trazodone 8 mg/kg or lactose placebo PO, 2 hours before departure from home; 7–14 day washout between visits
ECG Variables
ECG-derived HR (ECGHR), PR interval (mean of 5 consecutive beats), VVTI = ln[VAR(R1–R20)], HR-adjusted PRa
Statistics
Paired t-test or Wilcoxon signed-rank test; Spearman correlation; linear mixed-effects model for HR adjustment

HR Adjustment Formula

PRa = PR + 0.2751 × (ECGHR − 111)
β₁ = −0.2751 ms/bpm derived from linear mixed-effects model (p = 0.0020)
Reference ECGHR = 111 bpm (median of drug-free periods)

ECG-derived HR · PR interval · VVTI
  • Auscultated HR, RR, and FAS score did not differ significantly between treatments (all p > 0.05)
  • ECGHR was significantly higher under trazodone (median difference −12 bpm, p = 0.0007)
  • PR interval was significantly shorter under trazodone (mean difference 9.2 ms, p < 0.0001); all values remained within the reference range (60–130 ms) except one dog at ECGHR 200 bpm

05 · Clinical Implication

What does this mean?

Oral trazodone at 8 mg/kg shortened the PR interval in healthy conscious dogs, with no clinically significant arrhythmia observed.
The effect of trazodone on the PR interval should be considered when interpreting the ECG, as a HR-independent PR shortening persisted after heart rate adjustment.
The PR interval remained within the reference range in nearly all dogs and was not associated with adverse cardiac events.
